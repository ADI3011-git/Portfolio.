Philosophy

Supply chains should plan concurrently, not sequentially.

Most planning stacks still treat demand, supply, and execution as separate silos, each updated on its own clock. Kinaxis Maestro's concurrent planning model closes that gap and AI closes it further, reading signals, flagging exceptions, and simulating scenarios in real time instead of at the next batch run.

My work sits at that intersection: implementing the platform correctly first, then layering the intelligence that helps planners act faster than the disruption in front of them.

One connected plan, not three disconnected ones
Exceptions surfaced in minutes, not weeks
AI as a copilot for the planner, not a replacement
